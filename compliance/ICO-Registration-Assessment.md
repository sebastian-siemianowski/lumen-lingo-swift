## 1. Background

Under the Data Protection Act 2018, s. 25, most organisations that process personal data must pay a data protection fee to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO). Failure to register is a criminal offence under the DPA 2018.

## 2. Does LumenShore Need to Register?

### 2.1 Exemption Check

The ICO fee exemption applies only if the organisation:
- Processes personal data **only** for the purposes of staff administration (payroll, accounts), OR
- Processes personal data **only** for advertising, marketing, and public relations **in connection with its own business**, OR
- Processes personal data **only** for the maintenance of a public register

**LumenShore's data processing activities**:
- Newsletter/waitlist email collection (user email addresses)
- Website analytics (Vercel Analytics — aggregated, privacy-preserving)
- Error monitoring (Sentry — session replay with consent)
- iOS app data (on-device SwiftData, no server-side collection)
- Cookie consent records (localStorage)

### 2.2 Assessment Result

LumenShore processes personal data (email addresses) for newsletter communications and collects error/analytics data on users. This goes beyond the narrow exemptions listed above.

**Conclusion: LumenShore Limited IS required to register with the ICO.**

## 3. Fee Tier

The ICO data protection fee is tiered based on organisation size and turnover:

| Tier | Staff | Turnover | Annual Fee |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| Tier 1 (Micro) | Up to 10 staff | Up to £632,000 | £40 |
| Tier 2 (Small/Medium) | Up to 250 staff | Up to £36 million | £60 |
| Tier 3 (Large) | 250+ staff | £36 million+ | £2,900 |

**LumenShore's tier**: **Tier 1 (Micro)** — fewer than 10 employees, turnover under £632,000.

**Annual fee: £40**
